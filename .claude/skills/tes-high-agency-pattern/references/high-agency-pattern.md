# High-Agency Pattern

## Core Pattern

High-agency skills are conservative at activation and intense after activation:

1. explicit invocation only;
2. proactive and predictive posture after invocation;
3. evidence before asking when the repository can answer;
4. one question, decision, or risk at a time;
5. a recommended answer with every question;
6. cognitive brake honored immediately;
7. completion by sharper decision or durable knowledge;
8. no artifact unless something has actually resolved.

## Why It Works

The pattern improves precision because it removes accidental activation, reduces branch explosion, checks local evidence before asking the user, and keeps pressure reversible through a brake.

`tes-prospect` proves the plan-risk side: expose one hidden dependency or weak decision before execution.

`tes-mine` proves the language/evidence side: turn hidden terms, contradictions, and decisions into durable project knowledge only after they resolve.

## Adoption Gate

Before applying the pattern to another local skill, verify:

1. The workflow needs explicit activation.
2. The workflow benefits from proactive pressure after activation.
3. Evidence can answer some questions before the user is asked.
4. One question, decision, or risk at a time is enough to make progress.
5. A cognitive brake is needed because the skill can become intense.
6. Durable output appears only after something is resolved.

If any check fails, do not apply this pattern.

## Promotion Gate

Keep the pattern local until repeated use shows:

1. better precision;
2. lower contextual noise;
3. no weakening of `tes-mine` or `tes-prospect`;
4. no commercial claim needed to justify it;
5. clear evidence that a distributable surface would help users.
